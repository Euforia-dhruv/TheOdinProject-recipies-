Pav Bhaji

Ingredients

  1. For the Vegetable Blend (Bhaji):
    • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
    • 1 cup cauliflower florets
    • ½ cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
    • 1 medium carrot, chopped
    • ½ medium beetroot (grated or cubed - for natural red color)
  2. For the Masala
    • 3-4 tablespoons unsalted butter (or to taste)
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 1 cup tomatoes, finely chopped
    • ½ cup capsicum (green bell pepper), finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
    • 1.5 to 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji Masala
    • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
    • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
    • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander, finely chopped
    • Juice of ½ a lemon
  3. For the Pav (Buns):
    • 6-8 pav buns
    • Butter (for toasting)
    • A pinch of Pav Bhaji Masala and coriander leaves (optional, for the tawa)

Steps

Step 1: Boil and Mash the Vegetables

  1. Combine the potatoes, cauliflower, peas, carrot, and beetroot in a pressure cooker with 1 cup of water and a pinch of salt.
  2. Cook for 4 to 5 whistles (or until very soft).
  3. Once the pressure releases, use a potato masher to mash the boiled veggies into a coarse, chunky paste directly in the pot.

Step 2: Prepare the Bhaji Base

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil together in a large pan or tawa on medium heat.
  2. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for about a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the finely chopped capsicum and cook for 3 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, cooking until they become soft and pulpy.

Step 3: Combine and Simmer

  1. Add the Pav Bhaji Masala, Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and let the spices roast in the butter for about a minute.
  2. Add the mashed vegetables to the pan along with kasuri methi. Stir to combine completely.
  3. Add ½ to 1 cup of warm water to adjust the consistency. Use your masher again in the pan to further mash the mixture into a thick, smooth gravy.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 5-7 minutes until the flavors meld. Stir in the lemon juice and half of the chopped coriander.

Step 4: Toast the Pav

  1. Heat a flat tawa or skillet on medium heat and melt a generous amount of butter.
  2. Sprinkle a tiny pinch of Pav Bhaji Masala and fresh coriander on the butter.
  3. Slice the pav buns in half horizontally and place them on the butter. Press down lightly, adding more butter to the top, and toast until they are golden and slightly crisp.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

  1. Plate the hot bhaji in a bowl, topping it with a generous dollop of butter.
  2. Garnish with remaining chopped coriander and serve alongside the hot, buttery pav, a lemon wedge, and finely diced raw red onions.
