Chole bhature

Ingredients

  1. For the Dosa Batter
    • 1½ cups raw rice (like Sona Masuri)
    • ½ cup idli rice or parboiled rice
    • ½ cup urad dal (skinned black gram)
    • 2 tbsp chana dal (split bengal gram)
    • 1 tbsp toor dal (split pigeon peas)
    • 1 tsp methi seeds (fenugreek seeds)
    • ½ cup poha (flattened rice)
    • Salt to taste
    • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  2. For the Potato Masala (Aloo Bhaji)
    • 3 large potatoes (boiled, peeled, and roughly mashed)
    • 1 large onion (finely sliced)
    • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
    • 1 inch ginger (finely chopped)
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1 tsp urad dal & 1 tsp chana dal
    • A pinch of hing (asafoetida)
    • Few fresh curry leaves
    • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • 2 tbsp fresh coriander & 1 tbsp lemon juice (for finishing)

Steps

Step 1: Make the Batter

  1. Soak: Rinse and soak the rice with methi seeds in one bowl for 4-5 hours. In a separate bowl, rinse and soak the urad dal, chana dal, and toor dal for 2-4 hours.
  2. Grind: Drain the water and blend the dals into a smooth, fluffy paste. In a separate batch, blend the soaked rice with the poha into a slightly coarse paste.
  3. Mix & Ferment: Combine the rice and dal batters in a large bowl. Add salt, mix well, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours until it doubles in volume.

Step 2: Prepare the Potato Masala

  1. Temper: Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan on medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle. Add urad dal, chana dal, hing, and curry leaves. Sauté until the dals turn golden.
  2. Sauté: Add green chilies, ginger, and onions. Sauté until the onions shrink and turn translucent.
  3. Mash: Add the turmeric powder and salt. Toss in the boiled, mashed potatoes. Add a splash of water (about 2-3 tbsp) if the mixture is too dry. Mix and mash lightly for 2-3 minutes. Turn off the heat and stir in lemon juice and fresh coriander.

Step 3: Cook the Dosa

  1. Pour & Spread: Heat a heavy-bottomed cast-iron tava or non-stick griddle. Lightly oil the surface. Pour a ladle of batter in the center and quickly spread it in outward concentric circles to form a thin crepe.
  2. Crisp: Drizzle ½ tsp of oil or ghee around the edges and cook on medium-high heat until the bottom is golden and crispy.
  3. Serve: Place 2-3 tablespoons of the prepared potato masala in the center of the dosa, fold the sides over, and serve hot.
