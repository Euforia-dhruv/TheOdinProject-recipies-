Pani Puri

Ingredients

  1. For the Spiced Water
    • 1 cup fresh coriander leaves
    • ½ cup fresh mint leaves
    • 1-2 green chilies (adjust to spice preference)
    • 1 inch ginger
    • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp or lemon juice
    • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
    • 1 tsp black salt
    • Salt to taste
    • 3 to 4 cups of cold water
    • 2 tbsp boondi (fried chickpea flour balls)
  2. For the Potato Masala
    • 3 large potatoes (boiled, peeled, and mashed)
    • ½ cup boiled black chickpeas (kala chana) or white peas (optional)
    • ½ tsp cumin powder
    • ½ tsp red chili powder
    • Salt to taste
  3. Shells & Extras
    • 30-40 readymade puris
    • Sweet date-tamarind chutney (optional)
    • Finely chopped onions and sev (optional)

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Spiced Pani

  1. Add the coriander leaves, mint leaves, green chilies, ginger, and a splash of water to a blender and blend into a smooth paste.
  2. Transfer this green paste to a large bowl or pitcher.
  3. Add the tamarind pulp (or lemon juice), roasted cumin powder, black salt, and regular salt.
  4. Pour in 3 to 4 cups of cold water and stir well. Taste test and adjust salt or spice if needed.
  5. Stir in the boondi, then place the pani in the refrigerator to chill.

Step 2: Mix the Potato Filling

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the boiled and mashed potatoes with the boiled chickpeas.
  2. Add the cumin powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix everything together until it forms a chunky, uniform mixture.

Step 3: Assemble and Serve

  1. Gently crack open the top center of each puri using your thumb or a spoon to create a small hole.
  2. Stuff a teaspoon of the potato masala mixture into the puri.
  3. Add a pinch of chopped onions and a drop of sweet chutney, if using.
  4. Dunk the stuffed puri fully into your chilled, spiced pani and pop the whole thing into your mouth at once to enjoy the flavor explosion.
